Research

Currently, I'm trying to understand the E2-algebra structure of the Temperley-Lieb algebras. The Temperley-Lieb algebras are closely related braid groups, and they have a nice diagrammatic interpretation. They also satisfy homological stability. That they form an E2-algebra means that they're an algebra over the little cubes operad, or rather over its algebraic analogue. The rich E2-structure gives operations in homology, which one can hope to understand algebraically (or better yet, diagrammatically!). I also want to see if this point of view can lead to wider understanding of the unstable homology of the Temperley-Lieb algebras.
